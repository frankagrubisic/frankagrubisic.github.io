About

A polyglot from a young age, Franka would have been an English and Spanish major student, hadn’t it been for her burning interest in art and engineering. She is a senior year Computer Engineering student majoring in Multimedia Computing, with a background in geospatial engineering. Through her versatile experiences she developed an intense curiosity for the way people interact with each other and the world around them, and how technology and design can help facilitate this intercommunication and existence.

Her design method is defined by an unparalleled ability to integrate seemingly disparate realms and elements: art and engineering, purpose and aestheticism, the physical and the digital. She harnesses technology, inclusive research and design thinking to devise design propositions that feel familiar yet wondrous and that bring value for her clients and add meaning to the lives of the people she designs for.

By being active in a plethora of international organizations and by organizing conferences, she found immense value in working in interdisciplinary teams, and has arguably learned more doing so than in any other facet of her education. She likes working with future strategies and believes digital innovations should have a positive physical footprint.
